About Me

Hi, I am Sho. This is my personal website. I am currently a junior in University of Utah. I am majoring in Computer Science and Applied Mathematics.
I am interested in Probabilistic Machine Learning, optimization, efficient machine learning, and quantum computing. My goal is to become a researcher who pushes the boundaries of these fields and contributes to the research community
I am working as an undergraduate researcher in STARS Lab under Professor Suhas Prameela, doing computational molecular dynamics simulation focusing on alloys behavior under extreme condition accelerated by data-driven ML method.
I have developed an automated atomistic simulation workflow using ASE with Machine Learning Interatomic Potential (MLIP) to replace energy calculation by DFT. Also, training MLIP using dataset we generate by DFT calculation to make model that can learn large complex system.

Projects

1. BayesBench
In Progress - Building Bayesian optimization benchmark framework
BayesBench is a reproducible benchmark framework for studying sample-efficient optimization methods such as random search and Gaussian Process Bayesian Optimization. I am currently building the first benchmark pipeline using the Branin function, a standard black-box optimization test problem.
2. LoRA-QLoRA Studies
In Progress - Cleaning up code
This project studies parameter-efficient fine-tuning under free-Colab-scale compute. I manually implement LoRA layers in PyTorch, compare them with PEFT-based LoRA/QLoRA, and analyze how rank, target modules, and quantization affect memory, convergence, and validation loss.

Competitions & Hackathons

MIT iQuHACK — Quantum Computing Hackathon
February 2026
Participated in the BlueQubit Challenge, where I simulated large 50–60 qubit quantum circuits using Qiskit to identify hidden peak bitstrings and placed 24th. I optimized circuit simulation performance through gate reordering and canceling, reducing runtime by approximately 30%, and developed statistical candidate-selection heuristics to infer hidden peaks from near-uniform output distributions.
Crimson Hack — University of Utah Hackathon
October 2025
Built a physics-inspired rocket simulation game where players navigate from one planet to another while avoiding obstacles and debris. The game includes planet-specific gravity, fuel-limited movement mechanics, and interactive gameplay that allows users to select different solar-system planets and plan trajectories under changing physical conditions.
